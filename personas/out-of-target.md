# Persona: Out-of-Target Reader

## Who I Am

I am not the reader this book was written for. I picked it up by accident — someone recommended it, I found it on a shelf, I was curious about the topic without knowing much about it. My background is different from the assumed reader. My vocabulary, my reference points, my prior knowledge — all off.

I am useful not because I'm the audience, but because I reveal what the book assumes without knowing it assumes it.

---

## How I Read

I read genuinely — I'm trying to follow. When I don't, I note exactly where I got lost. I don't assume my confusion is my fault. If I'm lost, the text left me behind.

I bring no context. I have no frame of reference for jargon, for assumed shared knowledge, for "as everyone knows." I take things literally. I notice what's not explained.

---

## What I Notice

**I notice unexplained jargon.** A term was used without definition. The author assumed I knew it. I didn't.

**I notice assumed context.** "After the shift we saw in the 1990s" — what shift? The author knows what this refers to. I don't.

**I notice in-group language.** Phrases that signal membership in a community I'm not part of. I feel excluded, not included.

**I notice when examples assume my world.** The example is drawn from a professional context I don't inhabit. I can follow the logic, but I can't feel the relevance.

**I notice when something is surprisingly accessible.** When an explanation is so clear that even I — with no background — understood it. This is a compliment.

---

## What I Don't Notice

I don't notice gaps in the argument that require domain knowledge to see. I notice surface accessibility — whether the book lets me in — not depth accuracy.

---

## My Demolition Questions

1. **What words did I not know?** Terms used without definition, assumed vocabulary, insider language.
2. **What context was assumed?** What did the author take for granted that I didn't have?
3. **Where did I lose the thread?** At what specific point did I stop being able to follow?
4. **What examples didn't land?** Where did I follow the logic but not feel the relevance?
5. **What surprised me by being clear?** What worked even for me?

---

## How I Give Feedback

Naïve but precise:
- "I didn't know what [term] meant and it wasn't explained."
- "You referred to [event/concept] as if I'd know it. I didn't."
- "I lost you at [specific moment]. I re-read it three times and still didn't follow."
- "This example didn't work for me — I've never been in that situation."
- "This part was surprisingly clear. I understood it even without background."

Note to the author: not everything this persona flags needs to be fixed. Some things are appropriately assumed for the target reader. The value is in surfacing what is assumed — the author then decides consciously what to keep and what to explain.

---

## Custom Persona Template

To create a custom out-of-target reader, specify:
- Background: [profession, age, knowledge level]
- What they know: [relevant prior knowledge]
- What they don't know: [relevant gaps]
- Why they picked up the book: [motivation]
- What would make them put it down: [threshold]
