# Preset: Manual / How-To

## Identity
This book teaches something. The reader starts with a problem they can't solve and ends with the ability to solve it. Every chapter is a step in a transformation. The measure of success is not whether the reader was entertained or persuaded — it is whether they can now do something they couldn't do before.

---

## Vocabulary

| Generic term | This preset uses |
|---|---|
| Central claim | **The transformation** — who the reader is before and after this book |
| Chapter goal | **Skill or mindset shift** — what the reader can do after this chapter |
| Thesis | **The core insight** — the non-obvious truth that makes the method work |
| Argument | **Explanation + demonstration** — why this works, then proof that it does |
| Implication | **Application** — how the reader uses this in their specific context |
| Tension | **The obstacle** — what makes this hard, what the reader will resist |
| Structure | **Progression** — from foundational to advanced, from understanding to doing |

---

## ask-before-writing adaptations

Core question (replaces "what are you claiming?"):
> What can the reader do after reading this book that they cannot do now? Be specific — not "they'll be more productive" but "they'll be able to do X in Y context."

Reader question:
> What is the reader's biggest mistake or misunderstanding before they read this? What wrong belief do they arrive with?

Core insight question:
> What is the non-obvious thing — the insight that makes the whole method work — that most people in this field miss?

Structure question:
> What does the reader need to understand first before anything else makes sense? What is the foundational concept?

Obstacle question:
> What will the reader resist? Where will they want to skip ahead, cut corners, or argue that it doesn't apply to them?

Exercise question:
> What does the reader need to practice to make this real? What is the minimum viable action after each chapter?

---

## demolish adaptations

Primary attack vectors (in order of priority):
1. **Does it actually work?** — is the method proven, or is this anecdote and assertion?
2. **Context dependency** — does this work in all the situations the reader will apply it to, or only in specific conditions?
3. **The missing prerequisite** — what does the reader need to have in place before this works that the book hasn't addressed?
4. **The hard part** — has the book acknowledged where the method breaks down or requires exceptional skill?
5. **Generalizability** — is the author's experience typical, or are they an outlier presenting their exception as a rule?

Opening demolition frame:
> I'm going to read this as a skeptical practitioner who has seen many books promise transformation and deliver theory. I'll attack every claim that isn't earned by evidence or demonstration.

Key demolition questions:
> How do you know this works? What is the evidence?
> Under what conditions does this NOT work?
> What does the reader need to already have in place for this to be applicable?

## write adaptations

Chapter structure:
1. **The problem** — what is the reader struggling with right now?
2. **The wrong approach** — what do most people try, and why it fails
3. **The insight** — the non-obvious reframe that changes everything
4. **The method** — step by step, concrete and specific
5. **The practice** — the minimum action the reader takes after this chapter
6. **The check** — how the reader knows they've got it

Tone calibration:
- Respect the reader's intelligence but not their prior knowledge
- Be direct — every sentence should either teach something or set up teaching something
- Use examples that are specific enough to be real and general enough to be applicable
- Anticipate resistance: name the objection before the reader has to raise it

---

## consistency-check adaptations

Check for:
- **Progression logic** — does each chapter build on the previous, or could chapters be read in any order?
- **Promised skills** — does each chapter deliver the specific skill it claimed to teach?
- **Assumption tracking** — does a later chapter assume knowledge the book hasn't provided yet?
- **Tone consistency** — does the author maintain the voice of a trusted guide, or drift into lecturer or cheerleader?
- **Exercise coherence** — do the exercises actually practice what the chapter taught?
